Same Talent. Different Fates.

Have you ever wondered why a classmate with lower scores could study overseas and become a lawyer, while you couldn't? The reason is simple: their family could afford to game the system. And yours couldn't.

The "Rich Kid"

  • ✓Misses the local med school cut-off? They study overseas and still become a doctor.
  • ✓No local university offer? They simply get a degree abroad.

The "Poor Kid"

  • ✗Misses the cut-off? They're forced into a subject they're not passionate about.
  • ✗No local university offer? They settle for an Associate's Degree.

Ability is equal. Access isn't.

This Fight is Personal.

"I scored 3As and 2Bs on my A-levels, but I had to give up my dream of studying in the UK because my family couldn't afford the exam fees, let alone tuition. I watched classmates with lower grades get into better universities overseas simply because they had the financial means."

After a decade in the hedge fund industry, I couldn't shake that feeling of injustice. I left my career to found SocioBridge for one reason: to ensure the next generation of students has the fair shot I never did.

Jeremy W.

Founder, SocioBridge
